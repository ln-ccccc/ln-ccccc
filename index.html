About

Research before decoration.

When a multimodal model answers a spatial question correctly or incorrectly, can we identify which representation, grounding step, or reasoning mechanism produced that behavior?

I am an M.Sc. student in Geoinformation Engineering at the China University of Geosciences (Wuhan).

My main research direction is 3D multimodal intelligence: how models encode scenes, preserve geometry, ground language to objects, and reason about space.

I previously worked as an algorithm intern at DP Technology. My current workflow combines baseline reproduction, representation probing, controlled ablations, and system-level verification.

Research

Current questions.

The objective is not merely to increase an aggregate QA score. It is to identify which mechanism actually changed.

01

Representation probing

Measure what geometric information survives as object features move through projection layers and the LLM.

  • Layer-wise hidden-state probes
  • Relative-position decoding
  • Object-token mapping audits
02

2D–3D–LLM alignment

Study when alignment improves geometry, and when it only improves feature similarity without downstream gains.

  • 2D and 3D alignment targets
  • Retrieval and cosine diagnostics
  • Negative and shuffle controls
03

Verifiable spatial reasoning

Separate perception, grounding, and reasoning errors using explicit geometry and structured intermediate representations.

  • Scene graphs and geometry tools
  • Consistency checking
  • Grounded reasoning traces
04

Controlled reproduction

Reproduce representative 3D multimodal systems and test which components provide measurable causal gains.

  • Chat-Scene · LEO · SceneCOT
  • PointAlign · CLASP
  • Fixed splits, seeds, and checkpoints
